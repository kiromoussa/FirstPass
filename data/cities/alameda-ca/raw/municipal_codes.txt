MUNICIPAL CODE RESEARCH — Alameda, CA
Accessory Dwelling Units (detached ADU)
Compiled by the Municipal Code Researcher agent from official sources.

Section bodies are tagged with their source id in brackets, e.g. [S1].

### AMC §30-5.21(b) — Unit Size [S1]
The maximum floor area of a detached accessory dwelling unit shall not exceed
1,200 square feet of conditioned space. This maximum applies regardless of lot
size; smaller maximums established elsewhere in this chapter do not apply where
they would conflict with state law permitting an 800 square foot unit.

### AMC §30-5.21(c) — Side Setback [S3]
A minimum side setback of 4 feet shall be provided for an accessory dwelling
unit. No side setback is required for an existing structure converted to an ADU,
or for a new ADU constructed in the same location and to the same dimensions as
the structure it replaces.

### AMC §30-5.21(c) — Rear Setback [S3]
A minimum rear setback of 4 feet shall be provided for an accessory dwelling
unit. Like the corresponding side requirement, no rear setback is required for a
permitted conversion of existing permitted floor area.

### Alameda P&B — Submittal Checklist [S4]
A complete ADU submittal must include a site plan, a floor plan, building
elevations, and a Title-24 energy compliance report. Incomplete submittals are
returned without review. Electronic plan sets must be submitted as a single
combined PDF in addition to any DWG sheets.
