STATE CODE — California (applies in Los Angeles)
ADU standards that override local limits — Gov. Code §66310 et seq. / HCD
Representative excerpts compiled by the State Code Researcher agent.

### CA Gov. Code §66321 / HCD — Height (Detached) [S2]
A detached accessory dwelling unit may be up to 16 feet in height. The limit
increases to 18 feet where the parcel is within one-half mile walking distance of
a major transit stop or high-quality transit corridor, or supports an existing or
proposed multistory multifamily dwelling, with an additional two feet permitted to
align the roof pitch with the primary dwelling. A local agency may not impose a
height limit below this state floor.

### CA Gov. Code §66321 / HCD — Height (Attached) [S2]
An attached accessory dwelling unit may rise to 25 feet or the height limit that
applies to the primary dwelling under the local zoning code, whichever is lower.
An ADU is not required to be shorter than 16 feet.

### CA Gov. Code §66323 / HCD — Setbacks [S2]
No setback greater than 4 feet from the side and rear lot lines may be required
for an accessory dwelling unit that is not a conversion of existing space. No
setback may be required for an existing permitted structure or one constructed in
the same footprint.

### CA Gov. Code §66317 / HCD — Ministerial Approval [S2]
A permit application for an accessory dwelling unit shall be considered and
approved ministerially without discretionary review within 60 days of a complete
application. Failure to act within 60 days deems the application approved.
