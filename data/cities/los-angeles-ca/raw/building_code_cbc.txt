BUILDING CODE — California Building Code (CBC), Title 24 Part 2
Representative excerpts compiled by the Building Code Researcher agent.
Note: detached one- and two-family dwellings and ADUs are generally governed by
the California Residential Code (CRC); the CBC governs other occupancies and is
referenced for structural and fire-separation provisions.

### CBC §310 — Residential Occupancy Classification [S5]
Dwelling units are classified in Group R. A detached accessory dwelling unit
serving a one- or two-family dwelling is regulated under the California
Residential Code; where an ADU is within or attached to a larger Group R building,
the applicable Group R occupancy and fire-separation provisions of this code apply.

### CBC §705 — Exterior Walls and Fire Separation Distance [S5]
The fire-resistance rating of exterior walls and the allowable area of openings
are determined by the fire separation distance to the property line or to an
assumed imaginary line between buildings on the same lot. Walls with a fire
separation distance of less than 5 feet require a one-hour fire-resistance rating
and limited openings.

### CBC §1011 — Stairways [S5]
Stairways serving an occupied space shall have a minimum width, maximum riser
height of 7 3/4 inches, minimum tread depth of 10 inches, and handrails as
required. Refer to the Residential Code for stairways within dwelling units.
