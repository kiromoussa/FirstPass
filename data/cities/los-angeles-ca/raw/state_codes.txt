### Gov. Code § 66314 — ADU size and setback standards [B1]
Source: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=GOV&sectionNum=66314

"The total floor area for a detached accessory dwelling unit shall not exceed 1,200 square feet."

"The total floor area of an attached accessory dwelling unit shall not exceed 50 percent of the existing primary dwelling."

"A setback of no more than four feet from the side and rear lot lines shall be required for an accessory dwelling unit that is not converted from an existing structure."

### Gov. Code § 66321 — minimum unit size and height limits a local agency must allow [B1]
Source: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=GOV&sectionNum=66321

A local agency shall not establish a maximum square footage requirement that is less than:
"(A) Eight hundred fifty square feet of interior livable space."
"(B) One thousand square feet of interior livable space for an accessory dwelling unit that provides more than one bedroom."

A local agency shall not impose standards that do "not permit an accessory dwelling unit with at least 800 square feet of interior livable space and with four-foot side and rear yard setbacks to be constructed in compliance with all other local development standards."

Height limitations a local agency must allow (subdivision (b)(4)):
"(A) A height of 16 feet for a detached accessory dwelling unit on a lot with an existing or proposed single family or multifamily dwelling unit."
"(B) A height of 18 feet for a detached accessory dwelling unit on a lot with an existing or proposed single family or multifamily dwelling unit that is within one-half of one mile walking distance of a major transit stop or a high-quality transit corridor."
"(C) A height of 18 feet for a detached accessory dwelling unit on a lot with an existing or proposed multifamily, multistory dwelling."
"(D) A height of 25 feet or the height limitation in the local zoning ordinance that applies to the primary dwelling, whichever is lower, for an accessory dwelling unit that is attached to a primary dwelling."

### Gov. Code § 66322 — parking [B1]
Source: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=GOV&sectionNum=66322

A local agency shall not impose parking standards for an accessory dwelling unit when, among other conditions, the unit is "located within one-half of one mile walking distance of public transit", is part of the primary residence or an accessory structure, is in a historic district, or when on-street parking permits are required but not offered to the occupant.

### Gov. Code § 66323 — objective standards (ministerial approval) [B1]
Source: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=GOV&sectionNum=66323

"A total floor area limitation of not more than 800 square feet of livable space."
"rear yard and side setbacks of no more than four feet"
Height is incorporated by cross-reference: "A height limitation as provided in subparagraph (A), (B), or (C) of paragraph (4) of subdivision (b) of Section 66321, as applicable."
